## Goal
Eliminate `utils/` as a long-term destination by giving every helper an owner.

## Current repo cues
- `backend/app/utils/auth_middleware.py`
- `backend/app/utils/citation_utils.py`
- `backend/app/utils/claude_parsing_utils.py`
- `backend/app/utils/cost_tracking.py`
- `backend/app/utils/docx_utils.py`
- `backend/app/utils/file_utils.py`
- `backend/app/utils/path_utils.py`
- `backend/app/utils/presentation_export_utils.py`
- `backend/app/utils/source_content_utils.py`
- `backend/app/utils/text/`

## Scope
- Triage every file under `backend/app/utils/` and assign it to a real owner: `auth/`, `sources/`, `chat/`, `studio/`, `platform/`, or `base/`.
- Move generic file/document/export helpers into `platform/` or a feature-owned export package when the helper is feature-specific.
- Move source-specific helpers into `sources/`.
- Move chat/LLM parsing and cost helpers into `chat/` or `platform/llm/` depending on actual ownership.
- Move tiny, domain-neutral helpers into `base/` only if they are truly reusable and not just temporarily homeless.
- Leave temporary shims only where the migration still needs them.

## Out of scope
- Behavioral rewrites of helper logic.
- Creating a new generic dumping ground under another name.
- Deleting shims before all imports are migrated.

## Deliverables
- Rehomed helpers with clear owners.
- A near-empty or empty `backend/app/utils/` directory.
- Updated imports throughout the backend.

## Acceptance criteria
- Every former util has an explicit owner.
- `backend/app/utils/` is empty or contains only temporary shims scheduled for removal.
- No new vague helper bucket replaces `utils/`.

## Notes
This ticket should happen late, once the target domains already exist. Otherwise files will just get moved twice.
